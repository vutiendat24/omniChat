# OmniChat Presentation Script - About 10 Minutes

Good morning everyone. Today, I would like to present my project, OmniChat, an omnichannel chat management system designed to help businesses manage customer conversations from multiple platforms in one centralized workspace. In the current social commerce environment, customers do not contact a business through only one channel anymore. They may ask about a product on Facebook Messenger, send a message through Zalo Official Account, contact a shop on Shopee, or interact with a business through TikTok Shop. This behavior is very convenient for customers, but it creates many operational problems for businesses, especially small and medium-sized shops that need to reply quickly to convert conversations into orders.

Before having an application like OmniChat, businesses often manage messages manually across many different platforms. For example, one employee may open Facebook Page inbox, another employee may check Zalo OA, and another person may handle Shopee or TikTok messages. Every platform has its own interface, notification system, user identity, and message history. At first, this may seem manageable when the business receives only a few messages per day. However, when message volume increases, especially during marketing campaigns, livestreams, product launches, or flash sales, this manual workflow becomes very inefficient. Agents have to constantly switch between browser tabs and mobile applications. They may miss notifications, reply late, or even forget to answer some customers completely.

This creates several real-world difficulties. The first problem is missed messages. In social commerce, customers usually expect a fast response. If a customer asks about product price, shipping fee, size, stock availability, or promotion details, they often expect an answer within minutes. If the business replies too late, the customer may lose interest and buy from a competitor. The second problem is slow response time. When agents need to switch between many systems, they waste time finding the right conversation and checking previous messages. The third problem is duplicated or inconsistent work. Sometimes two employees may answer the same customer from different platforms without knowing that they are handling the same person. The fourth problem is the lack of centralized data. Managers cannot easily know how many conversations are coming from Facebook, how many are coming from Zalo, which agent is overloaded, which messages are still unassigned, or how fast the team is responding to customers.

Another important issue is customer context. A customer may contact the business on Facebook today and then continue the conversation on Zalo tomorrow. Without a centralized system, the agent may not realize that these messages belong to the same customer. As a result, the customer may need to repeat information such as their phone number, delivery address, previous product interest, or complaint details. This makes the service experience less professional. From the business perspective, fragmented customer history also makes it harder to build long-term relationships and evaluate customer value.

OmniChat is created to solve these problems by bringing all customer conversations into one unified inbox. The main idea is simple but powerful: instead of forcing agents to work across many separate platforms, OmniChat receives messages from different channels, normalizes them into one internal format, stores them centrally, and displays them in a single user interface. Agents can view conversations from Facebook, Zalo, and future channels in one place. They can click a conversation, read the full chat history, and reply directly from the OmniChat interface. The system then sends the reply back to the original platform through the correct external API.

For example, when a customer sends a message from Facebook Messenger, Facebook sends a webhook event to OmniChat. When another customer sends a message from Zalo Official Account, Zalo also sends a webhook event to OmniChat. These two platforms use different data formats, but OmniChat's Integration Service converts them into a standard internal event. This is one of the key design decisions of the system. Instead of making every internal service understand every external platform, OmniChat uses the Integration Service as an anti-corruption layer. It hides the complexity of Facebook, Zalo, Shopee, TikTok, and other platforms from the rest of the system.

The application is built using an event-driven microservices architecture. At a high level, external requests go through the API Gateway. The Integration Service receives webhooks from social platforms, validates the payload, checks for duplicate messages, and publishes standardized events to Kafka. Kafka works as the message backbone of the system. The Conversation Service consumes these events, creates or updates conversations, stores messages in the database, and publishes conversation events. The Routing Service listens for new conversation events and assigns conversations to suitable agents based on availability and workload. The WebSocket Service pushes realtime updates to the frontend so that agents can see new conversations or new messages without refreshing the page.

This architecture helps OmniChat solve the problem in a scalable and reliable way. Because services communicate through events, each service has a clear responsibility. The Integration Service focuses on external platforms. The Conversation Service focuses on conversations and messages. The Routing Service focuses on agent status, workload, and assignment. The Customer Service can manage customer profiles and channel identities. The WebSocket Service focuses on realtime communication with the agent interface. This separation makes the system easier to maintain and easier to extend when new channels are added in the future.

One of the most important features of OmniChat is the unified inbox. In the frontend design, the inbox page is the main workspace for agents. On one side, the agent can see a list of all conversations coming from multiple channels. Each conversation can show useful information such as the customer identity, the source channel, the conversation status, the last activity time, and the assigned agent. For example, a conversation may have a Facebook badge or a Zalo badge, so the agent immediately knows where the customer is coming from. When the agent clicks on a conversation, the main chat panel displays the conversation history between the customer and the business. Customer messages, agent messages, and system messages are shown clearly, so the agent can understand the context before replying.

Another core feature is message sending and receiving. OmniChat does not only collect messages; it also allows agents to respond from the same interface. When an agent sends a message, the frontend calls the Conversation Service API. The Conversation Service stores the agent message and publishes an event. The Integration Service then checks the channel connection of that conversation. If the conversation came from Facebook, the system calls the Facebook Send API. If the conversation came from Zalo, the system calls the Zalo Official Account API. This means the agent does not need to care about the technical differences between platforms. From the agent's point of view, they simply type a reply and click send.

OmniChat also supports conversation assignment and routing. In a real support team, not every conversation should be handled by the same person. Some agents may be online, some may be busy, and some may be offline. The Routing Service manages agent status and workload. When a new conversation arrives and it is not assigned to anyone, the system can assign it to an available agent. This helps distribute work more fairly and reduces the chance that new customers wait too long. Supervisors can also transfer conversations manually when needed. For example, if a customer has a technical issue, a supervisor can transfer the conversation to an agent who has more technical knowledge.

Realtime updates are another important feature. In a chat system, agents should not need to refresh the page repeatedly to check for new messages. OmniChat uses WebSocket to push events to the frontend. When a new message arrives, when a conversation is assigned, or when a conversation is transferred, the agent interface can update immediately. This improves the working experience and helps the team respond faster.

Reliability is also considered in the design. External platforms often retry webhook delivery when network conditions are unstable. If the system does not handle this carefully, the same message may be saved multiple times. OmniChat uses idempotency checks with Redis to avoid duplicate processing. The system stores message identifiers temporarily and ignores duplicate webhook events. This ensures that customer conversations remain clean and accurate.

In terms of user roles, OmniChat can support different types of users. Agents are the main users who handle assigned conversations and reply to customers. Supervisors can monitor team activity, view conversations, and transfer chats when needed. Admins can manage channel connections, configure routing rules, and later view reports. Customers do not use OmniChat directly, but they benefit from faster and more consistent service because the support team works more efficiently.

The main functions of the application include receiving messages from multiple channels, displaying all conversations in a centralized inbox, showing conversation history, allowing agents to send replies, managing agent status, assigning or transferring conversations, and supporting realtime updates. In the future, the system can be expanded with more advanced features such as quick replies, conversation tags, SLA alerts, customer profile management, customer profile merging, and analytics dashboards. These features can help businesses improve productivity and measure service quality more accurately.

The benefits of OmniChat are clear. For agents, it reduces the need to switch between many applications and helps them focus on customer support. For supervisors, it provides better visibility into team workload and conversation status. For admins and business owners, it creates a centralized data source for future reporting and decision-making. For customers, it creates a faster and smoother support experience. Most importantly, for the business, OmniChat helps reduce missed chats, improve response time, increase conversion opportunities, and create a more professional customer service process.

In conclusion, OmniChat solves a very practical problem in modern social commerce. Businesses today communicate with customers through many channels, but managing these conversations separately is inefficient and risky. OmniChat turns scattered messages into one manageable workflow. By combining a unified inbox, event-driven microservices, smart routing, realtime updates, and platform integrations such as Facebook and Zalo, the application helps businesses respond faster, manage agents better, and deliver a more consistent customer experience. The final goal of OmniChat is not only to build a chat interface, but to build a centralized operational platform for customer conversations. Thank you for listening.
